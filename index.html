  • Our tours

    Luxor - East Bank

    Join me on a half-day trip to Luxor's East Bank.
    Your trip will begin with a visit to the Karnak Temple, one of the largest temple complexes in the world. Built over a period of 2000 years, the temple is an impressive display of ancient Egyptian architecture and art
    Next, you will visit the Luxor Temple, another impressive ancient site located in the heart of the city. Built during the New Kingdom period, the temple is known for its beautiful carvings and hieroglyphics. You will also have the opportunity to see the Avenue of the Sphinxes, a unique and impressive feature of the temple complex.
    In between exploring the temples, you can choose to take a trip on the Nile River by a small, private boat to see the city from a different perspective.

  • Our tours

    Luxor - West Bank

    Join me on a half-day trip to Luxor's West Bank.
    Your trip will begin with a visit to the Valley of the Kings, where you'll have the opportunity to explore the tombs of some of ancient Egypt's most powerful rulers, including Tutankhamun and Rameses the Great.
    Next, you'll visit Medinet Habu, the mortuary temple of Pharaoh Rameses III. This massive temple is known for its well-preserved relief carvings, which depict scenes of battle and daily life in ancient Egypt.
    After that, you'll head to the Temple of Queen Hatshepsut, one of the most significant examples of ancient Egyptian architecture. The temple's iconic three-level terraces are carved into the cliff face, and offer stunning views of the surrounding area.
    Your final stop will be the Colossi of Memnon, two massive stone statues that once guarded the entrance to Amenhotep III's mortuary temple. These imposing figures are some of the few remaining structures from the temple, which was largely destroyed over the centuries.

    Dendera

    Join us on a one-day tour of the ancient Egyptian temple complex of Dendera.
    The tour begins with a short drive to Dendera where you will have the chance to explore the temple of Hathor. I suggest doing it on your own pace, marveling at its beautifully preserved hieroglyphics, carvings, and reliefs.
    After visiting the temple of Hathor, you will have some time to explore the Roman-era Birth House also known as Mammisi which offers fascinating glimpse into the beliefs and practices of the ancient Egyptians.
    To wrap up our tour you will visit the Temple of Osiris. The temple is famous for its well-preserved columns and ceiling, which are decorated with intricate carvings and hieroglyphics. As it is dedicated the god of the underworld it features inscriptions that provide insights into the ancient beliefs about the afterlife.
